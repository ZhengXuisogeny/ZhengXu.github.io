Introduction to Isogeny Hard Problems (Updating)

Isogeny Hard Problems

There are two hard problems related to supersingular elliptic curves: one is computing an isogeny between randomly given supersingular elliptic curves; the other is computing the endomorphism ring of a randomly given supersingular elliptic curve. Additionally, the structure of the supersingular isogeny graph determines the randomness of isogeny walks, which affects the security of cryptographic schemes based on these hard problems.

Computing Isogenies Problem

  1. Classical Attack: Meet-in-the-Middle (2016)

    Christina Delfs and Steven D. Galbraith proposed a meet-in-the-middle attack to recover isogenies with a time complexity of \(\tilde{O}(\sqrt{p})\).

    Computing Isogenies Between Supersingular Elliptic Curves Over \(\mathbb{F}_p\)

  2. Quantum Attack: Grover + Kuperberg (2014)

    Jean-Francois Biasse, David Jao and Anirudh Sankar proposed a method that uses Grover's algorithm to compute isogenies over \(\mathbb{F}_p\) and then leverages Kuperberg's algorithm for solving the Hidden Shift Problem, finding isogenies over \(\mathbb{F}_p\) in subexponential time with an overall time complexity of \(\tilde{O}(p^{1/4})\).

    A Quantum Algorithm for Computing Isogenies Between Supersingular Elliptic Curves

  3. Quantum Attack: Claw Finding (2009)

    Seiichiro Tani proposed a claw-finding method applicable to isogenies, with a time complexity of \(\tilde{O}(A^{1/3})\) for computing an isogeny of degree \(A\).

    Claw Finding Algorithms Using Quantum Walk

Computing Endomorphism Rings Problem

  1. Classical Attack (2020)

    Kirsten Eisenträger, Sean Hallgren, Chris Leonardi, Travis Morrison and Jennifer Park proposed a method to generate endomorphism rings using two endomorphisms of a supersingular elliptic curve, with a time complexity of \(O(\sqrt{p}\log^2(p))\).

    Computing Endomorphism Rings of Supersingular Elliptic Curves and Connections to Path-finding in Isogeny Graphs

  2. Quantum Attack (2020)

    For quantum attacks, similar to computing isogenies, the complexity is \(\tilde{O}(p^{1/4})\).

Equivalence of Hard Problems

  1. KLPT Algorithm (2014)

    David Kohel, Kristin Lauter, Christophe Petit and Jean-Pierre Tignol first proposed the KLPT algorithm, which finds an equivalent smooth norm ideal given an ideal. Thus, with a known endomorphism ring, applying the KLPT algorithm to connecting ideals yields smooth norm ideals, which can then be converted to isogenies, reducing the isogeny computation problem to the endomorphism ring computation problem.

    On the Quaternion \(\ell\)-Isogeny Path Problem

  2. Reduction of Hard Problems (2018)

    Kirsten Eisentrager, Sean Hallgren, Kristin Lauter, Travis Morrison and Christophe Petit established the equivalence between the isogeny computation problem and the endomorphism ring computation problem.

    Supersingular Isogeny Graphs and Endomorphism Rings: Reductions and Solutions

  3. Equivalence of Computing Endomorphism Rings and Non-trivial Endomorphisms (2024)

    Aurel Page and Benjamin Wesolowski showed that computing the endomorphism ring of a supersingular elliptic curve is equivalent to computing a non-trivial endomorphism, reducing SQIsign to this hard problem.

    The Supersingular Endomorphism Ring and One Endomorphism Problems are Equivalent

  4. Attacking CSIDH with Known Full Endomorphism Rings (2024)

    Wouter Castryck, Lorenz Panny and Frederik Vercauteren used twists to show that CSIDH can be broken in polynomial time with knowledge of the endomorphism ring of a supersingular elliptic curve over an algebraic closure, reducing CSIDH to the endomorphism computation problem.

    Rational Isogenies from Irrational Endomorphisms

Isogeny Graphs

  1. \(\mathbb{F}_p\)-Isogeny Graphs (2016)

    Christina Delfs and Steven D. Galbraith described the structure of isogeny graphs of supersingular elliptic curves over finite fields \(\mathbb{F}_p\).

    Computing Isogenies Between Supersingular Elliptic Curves Over \(\mathbb{F}_p\)

  2. Graph Structure of Curves with Trace \(0, \pm p\) (2018)

    Gora Adj, Omran Ahmadi, Alfred Menezes described the isogeny graph structure of curves with trace \(0, \pm p\) and determined the number of cycles in the \(\ell\)-isogeny graph of curves with trace \(\pm 2p\) for supersingular elliptic curves with \(j\)-invariant 1728.

    On Isogeny Graphs of Supersingular Elliptic Curves Over Fnite Fields

  3. \(\mathbb{F}_p\)上的超奇异椭圆曲线在同源图中的结构（2019,2020年）

    Songsong Li, Yi Ouyang, Zheng Xu 给出了\(\ell\)-同源图中\(j\)-不变量在\(\mathbb{F}_p\)上的超奇异椭圆曲线的环路和邻域个数。

    Loops of Isogeny Graphs of Supersingular Elliptic Curves at \(j = 0\)

    Neighborhood of the Supersingular Elliptic Curve Isogeny Graph at \(j = 0\) and \(1728\)

    Endomorphism Rings of Supersingular Elliptic Curves Over \(\mathbb{F}_p\)

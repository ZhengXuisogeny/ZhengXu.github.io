Abel Varieties(Updating)

Abel Varieties

Recently, the focus of research in isogeny-based cryptography has largely moved to abelian varieties, and techniques related to Abelian varieties have become a hot issue in isogeny-based cryptography.

High-dimensional Isogeny-based Cryptography

  1. CDS-Hash Function（2020）

    Wouter Castryck, Thomas Decru, and Benjamin Smith proposed a new hash function constructed using two-dimensional Abelian varieties.

    Hash Functions From Superspecial Genus-2 Curves Using Richelot Isogenies

  2. Two-dimensional SIDH(2019)

    E. V. Flynn and YanBo Ti proposed two-dimensional SIDH.

    Genus Two Isogeny Cryptography

  3. MIKE（2025）

    Damien Robert derived new abelian varieties by leveraging the action of finitely presented torsion-free modules (rank 2) on supersingular elliptic curves (whereas actions of finitely presented projective modules are required for general abelian varieties). This action eliminates the need to transmit image points and, due to its module-based (rather than ideal-based) nature, resists Kuperberg's quantum attacks. Based on this, he constructed the module action-based key exchange protocol MIKE.

    The Module Action For Isogeny Based Cryptography

Mathematical Problems and Algorithms Related to Cryptography on Abelian Varieties

  1. Determine Whether the Image Curve of a High-dimensional Isogeny is a Product of Elliptic Curves(2024)

    Maria Corte-Real Santos, Craig Costello and Sam Frengley presented an algorithm for determining whether a high - dimensional isogeny image curve is a product of elliptic curves or the Jacobian of a hyperelliptic curve.

    An Algorithm for Efficient Detection of (N,N)-Splittings and Its Application to the Isogeny Problem in Dimension 2

  2. KLPT\\(^2\\) (2025)

    Wouter Castryck, Thomas Decru, Peter Kutas, Abel Laval, Christophe Petit, and Yan Bo Ti proposed a two-dimensional KLPT algorithm, which obtains a method for finding equivalent ideals by computing matrices in high-dimension.

    KLPT\\(^2\\):Algebraic Path-finding in Dimension Two and Applications

Isogeny Graph of Abelian Varieties

  1. Richelot Isogeny Graph(2020)

    Toshiyuki Katsura and Katsuyuki Takashima presented Richelot isogeny graph. They determined whether the image curve of a (2,2)-isogeny was a product of elliptic curves or the Jacobian of a hyperelliptic curve by checking whether the reduced automorphism group contained a long-order 2 element, and gave the number of spliting isogenies.

    Counting Richelot Isogenies Between Superspecial Abelian Surfaces

  2. High-dimensional Isogeny Graph (2021)

    Bruce W. Joran and Yevgeny Zaytman provided the definition of high-dimensional isogeny graphs. They proved that high-dimensional isogeny graphs are connected by strong approximation, though the isogeny graphs are not necessarily Ramanujan graphs.

    Isogeny Graphs of Superspecial Abelian Varieties and Brandt Matrices

  3. Specific Classification of Richelot Isogeny Graphs (2021)

    Enric Florit and Benjamin Smith proposed a classification of the types of image curves obtained from different (2, 2)-isogenies by the action of the automorphism group on the kernel of (2, 2)-isogenies.

    Automorphisms and Isogeny Graphs of Abelian Varieties, with Applications to the Superspecial Richelot Isogeny Graph

    An Atlas of the Richelot Isogeny Graph

  4. Lopps and Neighborhoods of High-Dimensional Isogeny Graphs (2025)

    Zheng Xu, Yi Ouyang, and Zijian Zhou gave the number of loops and adjacent edges for abelian varieties of the product type of supersingular elliptic curves, one of which is defined over \\(\mathbb{F}_p\\).

    Neighborhood of Vertices in the Isogeny Graph of Principally Polarized Superspecial Abelian Surfaces
